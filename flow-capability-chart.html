Stuck? Here's the method.

Don't start with the average — it will lie to you. Start with the shape. The bars tell you where the bulk of the work lands; the cumulative line tells you where the tail begins. Find where the line flattens out, then filter by dependency and watch which one drags that tail to the right. That's your answer, and it's the same move you'd make in a retrospective with the team in the room.

About

Shape to cause,
in seconds.

The Flow Capability Chart is a frequency distribution with a cumulative percentage overlay, paired with rich filtering. The bars show the shape of the work. The cumulative line shows where the long tail begins. The filters get the team from shape to cause in seconds. It is the visual companion to the methodology in Building the Enterprise in Flow, designed to drive guided retrospectives.

Get the visual

Download and import.

Download the Flow Capability Chart, then import it into Power BI Desktop: open the Visualizations pane, click the ··· (more options) menu, choose Import a visual from a file, and select the downloaded .pbiviz file.

Your data stays in your report

The Flow Capability Chart runs inside Power BI's sandboxed visual container. It makes no external network calls, collects no telemetry, and sends nothing off your machine. It works with imported data and needs no internet connection. The full source is on GitHub if you want to verify it yourself.
